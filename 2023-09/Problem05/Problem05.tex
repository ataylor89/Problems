Problem 5: Find $\cos\left(\dfrac{3\pi}{2} + \theta\right)$ in terms of $\sin \theta$ or $\cos \theta$. (Source: AoPS Precalculus Ex. 3.1.4)

Solution:

\begin{align*}
\cos(\frac{3\pi}{2}. + \theta) &= \cos \frac{3\pi}{2} \cos \theta - \sin \frac{3\pi}{2} \sin \theta \\
&= 0 - (-1 \cdot \sin \theta) \\
&= 0 - (- \sin \theta) \\
&= \boxed{\sin \theta}
\end{align*}
